Environmental Justice Benefits & Burdens Analysis

  • Project Description
  • Every four years, a Metropolitan Planning Organization, such as the SEDA-COG Metropolitan Planning Organization (MPO), must develop a Transportation Improvement Program (TIP); a listing of upcoming, multi-modal transportation projects (i.e. highways bridges, public transit, bike/pedestrian) within the MPO's borders. An important aspect of the TIP is to ensure that transportation funding is avoiding disproportionately high and adverse human health and environmental effects, including social and economic effects, on minority populations and low-income populations.

    To guarantee that Environmental Justice (EJ) standards were being upheld for the 2025-2028 SEDA-COG MPO TIP, I was tasked with mapping the upcoming transportation projects along with United States Census- American Community Survey data to identify any benefits and burdens to the SEDA-COG population. A demographic analysis was also conducted for this project. A 30-page report documenting my findings along with an official EJ map viewer were created and submitted to PennDOT for the TIP.

    The maps created for the report were made in ArcGIS Pro. Tables and other graphics were created in Microsoft Excel and Microsoft PowerPoint. The report itself was created in Microsoft Word.

    The online map tool displayed below was created in ArcGIS Experience Builder. A swipe tool was added to view the EJ population polygons at the same time without having to change layer views. One can click on a point on the map to learn more about the programmed transportation project.
